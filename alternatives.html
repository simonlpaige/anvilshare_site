Anvilshare vs the SaaS incumbents

ServiceTitan, Jobber, Mitchell1, Buildium, and the rest

These platforms work. They also bill a 12-truck HVAC shop the same way they bill a 200-truck regional. You use about 20 percent of the features and pay 100 percent of the price.

Pick the incumbent if you need every feature, you have a corporate office that uses the reporting suite, or compliance forces a name-brand vendor on you.

Pick Anvilshare if the monthly bill is over $200, you only use a slice of what the platform does, and you want software that fits your shop instead of the other way around.

Anvilshare vs Zapier or Make stitched together

The duct-tape approach

You can usually replace 30 to 50 percent of a SaaS bill by stitching Zapier, Airtable, Notion, and a few Make scenarios. We respect the move. It also tends to break in subtle ways, get expensive past 5,000 tasks a month, and become a one-person job that nobody else on the team can read.

Pick the stitched setup if you have a strong tinkerer on your team, your data volume is low, and you do not need anyone else to maintain it.

Pick Anvilshare if the duct tape has already broken once, you want one system instead of seven, and you need someone to call when it breaks at 4pm Friday.

Anvilshare vs hiring a contract developer

The custom-build path

A good contract developer can build you exactly what you want. They cost $80 to $200 an hour, take longer than they quoted, and usually leave you with code that only they understand. If they move on, you start over.

Pick a contract dev if you have a clear spec, deep pockets, and someone in-house who can take the handoff.

Pick Anvilshare if you do not want to write the spec yourself, you want to pay out of savings instead of cash up front, and you want a system that other operators in your vertical have already used.

Anvilshare vs a generic AI consultancy

The AI strategy shop

They run a workshop, deliver a deck, and propose a six-figure roadmap. Sometimes useful for a 500-person company. Almost never the right shape for a 12-truck shop.

Pick a consultancy if you are in a regulated enterprise that needs a vendor on a master agreement, or you need a slide deck for a board.

Pick Anvilshare if you would rather have working software in 2 to 4 weeks than a 60-page strategy deck.

When we are the wrong call

We will say no to your project if any of these are true:

  • Your software handles regulated medical, legal, or banking data as the primary use case.
  • You need 24-hour follow-the-sun support from day one.
  • You are looking for a polished self-serve SaaS, not a built-for-you replacement.
  • Your monthly SaaS bill is under $200 a month. The math does not work for either of us.
  • You need every feature of the incumbent, including the ones you do not currently use.

If any of those describe your situation, the honest answer is to stay on the incumbent or hire a different shop.
