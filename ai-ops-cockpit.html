Home / AI Ops Cockpit

An AI operations cockpit that remembers what matters and watches the boring stuff for you.

Most founders already use ChatGPT or Claude. The problem is the chat forgets, the files scatter, and the boring follow-ups still fall through. The cockpit is a private setup that keeps the memory in one place, runs scheduled checks, and texts you when something needs a human.

What you get

  • A private chat channel on your phone, tied to your business memory.
  • A local-first memory folder so your context lives with you, not in someone's training set.
  • A simple dashboard that shows what ran today and what it found.
  • Five starter automations, set up for your specific business.
  • Weekly health check for the first 30 days.
  • A handoff guide so you can keep running it without us.

The five starter automations

  1. Morning brief. What matters today, what is stuck, what needs a decision.
  2. Open-loop tracker. Who owes what, when, and what the next move is.
  3. Website monitor. Broken pages, broken forms, uptime, visible regressions.
  4. Lead brief. Facts, fit score, and a suggested next step before any outreach.
  5. Weekly review. Shipped work, decisions, failures, next bets.

Who this is for

  • Founder-led companies with messy internal workflows.
  • Agencies and consultants juggling many clients.
  • Small operators who already use ChatGPT or Claude and want it to actually remember.
  • Privacy-aware teams that want their context kept local.

Who this is not for, yet

  • Regulated medical or legal workflows.
  • Payroll or banking documents as the first use case.
  • Enterprise procurement processes.
  • Anyone who expects polished self-serve SaaS.

How a pilot runs

  1. 30-minute scoping call. We agree on the five automations that matter most.
  2. Setup week. We install the cockpit, wire the memory folder, and connect the automations.
  3. 30 days of weekly health checks. Tweak what is noisy, harden what is useful.
  4. Handoff. You keep running it. We are around if you need us.

Why this exists

Anvilshare's bigger play is rewriting expensive SaaS for small businesses and splitting the savings. The cockpit is the lighter wedge. Installing it gives both sides a 30-day window to see how the work actually flows, and reveals which paid software is the real expensive leak. After the pilot, the upsell is honest: we have been watching the work for 30 days, here is the system that is actually worth replacing.

If you only ever buy the cockpit, that is fine. It pays for itself on the time it saves you in the first month.
