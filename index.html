fig 01 / the math

The boring math.

The median customer pays for nine SaaS tools and opens three. The other six are autopay you forgot about, charging four figures a year for tabs nobody clicks.

An audit walks through the line items, marks what's actually load-bearing, and shows what a replacement would cost. The first number is usually a surprise.

We didn't set out to build a smaller stack. We set out to build one where the tools answer questions instead of asking them. The smaller stack is a side effect, the way a good shop has fewer hammers than a bad one.

fig 03 / what we replace

What's on the shelf.

  • Dispatch software

    The scheduling and routing tools your office manager fights with every morning. The most expensive line on the statement, and usually the easiest to replace.

  • Field service management

    Job tracking, work orders, the mobile app the techs use in the field. Most shops use a third of the features and pay for all of them.

  • Invoicing

    Billing, payment collection, the QuickBooks integration that never quite worked. A replacement that actually syncs gives you back an hour or two each week.

  • Scheduling

    Appointment booking, calendar management, customer notifications. Simple enough that a custom build costs a fraction of the subscription.

  • Point of sale

    Counter and field payment processing. If your POS takes more than 2% of transactions plus a flat monthly fee, it's worth a look.

  • Inventory and basic CRM

    Parts tracking, customer records, service history. The kind of thing a spreadsheet almost handles but shouldn't have to.

fig 04 / the brain

And one tool that comes with every build.

Every replacement we ship includes a private business brain. It's a chat assistant that reads your customer list, your past quotes, your invoices, your notes. Ask it "who haven't we heard from in 90 days" and get an answer in seconds.

It runs on your hardware, not ours. The folder is plain text on disk. Open it, read it, hand it to anyone competent. Costs are small and visible on the bill.

It's not the same as a chatbot you've used before. The chatbot you've used before forgets you between sessions. This one doesn't.
